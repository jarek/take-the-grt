* How do we deal with the routes that have many deviations (such as 7A,B,C, etc)? Should we treat them as separate routes? (tsyd)
* Should we include all of the special routes? (tsyd)
* Do we care about arrival times or just departure? (tsyd)

These messy routes are, as far as I know, 7 and 8 (not sure if Cambridge has something similar). When I was looking at the GTFS spec, it seemed to me that we can accomodate the A,B,C,D,E stuff reasonably easily by changing which stops a trip stops at. ISTR it's also possible to change a trip's headsign to indicate the variation. Nothing's stopping us from defining these as separate routes, but that might make trip planning a bit more confusing?

As for accomodation within our model, I have specifically not looked into that yet, singling this out as a problem to solve later. Searching through a route's trips to find routes with given headsigns is slightly insane, so we might want a sub-route designation field that would later get integrated into the GTFS output. As I said, I haven't thought about this extensively yet.

The special routes (busplus, etc) will get included eventually, but I'm not treating them as high priority right now.

For most of the stoptimes we GRT doesn't publish an arrival time, and I would really hesistate before defining one on our own. Arrival times may come into play in situations like an end of a run or a layover at Charles St. I haven't given it much thought, it can certainly be done better but for now, when we do have the data, we should approximate/alter/disregard as seems appropriate. (Basically, no hard and fast rules ATM.) --Qviri 06:42, 2 February 2009 (UTC)
