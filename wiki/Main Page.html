Welcome to the Take the GRT website. Our comprehensive mission statement / documentation is on our about page, while the information aimed at programmers is at developers. Below is a 15-second version for your enjoyment:

[edit] What?

Take the GRT is a project aiming to put Grand River Transit's rider information data into a format that can be easily used by computer programs.

We will be focusing on the bus schedules as our first priority. Currently, the schedules are only available online as large PDF files, and the data cannot be extracted easily.

We want to make the information easily available to everyone to use as they wish. Applications using the data will be created by third parties, although we hope to be able to offer help.

[edit] Why?

The current PDF schedules take very long to download and open on older or slower computers. This makes it difficult for some users to view the schedules, and almost completely rules out any mobile/handheld devices from viewing the schedules.

The information is not easily extractable. This means that any third-party application wishing to display or use GRT schedules must jump through large hoops. A couple of example applications are apps for cell phones and devices equipped with GPS that show the nearest stop and time the bus will leave and desktop widgets for any operating system or application that display next departures from a user's usual route and stop.

[edit] Where?

In the Waterloo Region and right here online.

[edit] Who?

Take the GRT is a a Waterloo Region-based community of volunteers; the idea is a brainchild of user:Qviri, who is for now the acting head.

Email us at admin@takethegrt.ca. All emails will be read, and we'll do our best to reply.

[edit] How?

Through a community effort, some brains, some laptops, and too little sleep.

[edit] When?

Starting now, and helping you in the near future. Stay tuned.
